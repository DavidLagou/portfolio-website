PHASE ONE

Identifying The Issue

For a class assignment we needed to take an existing website and create a case study for it. I chose the Yale School Of Arts website for its interesting choice of design. Although it states that the website lets students, faculty and staff submit articles, I do believe that these freedoms should have come with more parameters.

The yale school of arts is a graduate school that offers MFAâ€™s in graphic design, painting/printmaking photography and sculpture. They Are located in New Haven Connecticut and have approximately 126 students.

PHASE TWO

Pain Points

I started with going through the website and writing down some of the most prominent pain points that could affect the end users journey .

  1. The biggest pain point I found while scrolling through the website was the lack of symmetry found on almost every page. This made the website feel less professional and made me question the legitimacy of the content.
  2. A very distracting color theme made it hard to focus and read content on the website. I spent more time analyzing the website aesthetics than I did trying to read the content. For the end user, this will not only waste their time but anger them along the way.
  3. Confusing navigational labeling made it hard for me to find my way throughout the website.

PHASE FOUR

Persona

In order to get a grasp on who the target audience will be for the website, I have decided to create a persona. Amanda Smith, like my brother, was a high school student ready to apply for universities. She has a high interest in Arts, and would like to graduate with a degree in an arts program.

Some other users to consider were parents, faculty, and the general public.

PHASE SEVEN

Journey Map

I then decided to illustrate the possible journey that Amanda would take from the beginning of her university hunt, all the way through to acceptance. Also included are some of the problems she might face along the way. This is to better understand the process of the user and discover any challenges that the user may encounter along the way.
